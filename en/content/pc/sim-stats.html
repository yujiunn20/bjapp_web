Sim & Stats

Sim & Stats runs Monte Carlo simulations via a Python backend. Configure hand count, table rules, counting system, and Bet Ramp to generate long-term data comparing EV, ROI, and variance across strategies and rule sets.

Sim

The Sim page is where you configure and launch simulations. The Python engine runs in the background and can handle large hand counts.

  • Hand Count: Set how many hands to simulate.
  • Table Rules: Configure H17 / S17, deck count, penetration, Double rules, and more to model different table conditions.
  • Counting System: Choose Basic, Hi-Lo, or another system for the simulation.
  • Bet Ramp: Set True Count thresholds and multipliers to observe how betting strategy affects long-term outcomes.

Results are saved as statistics and viewable in Stats after the simulation completes.

Stats

The Stats page displays simulation results for comparing long-term performance across configurations.

  • Net Profit & ROI: See overall profitability of a strategy across the simulated hand count.
  • EV & Variance: Understand expected value and standard deviation to evaluate strategy stability.
  • Multi-Run Comparison: Run multiple simulations with different settings and compare them side by side in Stats.
Simulation results show how strategies, rules, Bet Ramp, and variance interact — they do not guarantee the same outcome in live play. Blackjack edges are small and short-term variance is large; running the same configuration multiple times helps evaluate the stability of the result distribution.
